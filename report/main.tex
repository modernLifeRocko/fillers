\begin{abstract}
  Anyone who has watched anime for enough time has complained about the abundance of filler in some anime.
  We analyze their frequency in anime, how it is affected by length of series, and their evolution through time.
  We also speculate about the future of filler in anime and their effect on quality of the series.
\end{abstract}
  
\section{Filler Data}
\subsection{What is filler?}
Anime is usually an adaptation of material in other media.
Filler refers to episodes that don't follow the original material.
They do not move the plot forward.

While completely original anime do exist, they are the exception rather than the rule.

\subsection{Source of filler data}
We take the data about fillers from \href{www.animefillerlist.com}{Anime Filler List}.
For each anime in its database, we get a classification of the episodes in one of four categories: Manga Canon, Anime Canon, Mixed Canon/Filler, and Filler.In addition, we get some meta data like release dates.

We are interested in getting the following information for each anime:
\begin{description}
  \item[Release Year:] The year the first episode aired.
    For multi-year shows, this biases our data to earlier years.
    However, we believe that this aptly represents the way shows are run in regards to filler.
    For example, during the run of Naruto Shippuden it became more common for anime to be released in seasons, but the show maintained its continuous mode of airing.
  \item[Percentage of filler:] For our purposes, we consider Manga and Anime Canon episodes as $0\%$ filler, mixed episodes as $50\%$ filler, and Filler episodes as $100\%$ filler.
  We will not make differences between Anime and Manga Canon.
  \item[Length in episodes:] Total of episodes, all counted regardless of their filler tag.
\end{description}
